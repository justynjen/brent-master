The Anatomy of an Exchange

The properties you can choose from with Stratton Investments are home to credit worthy clients in long-term leases that have already passed our due-diligence process.A 1031 Exchange or TIC 1031 exchange consists of 4 parts:

"The 1031 Exchanger" has property that they are reliquishing and makes an agreement with a Qualified Intermediary to facilitate the 1031 exchange.

When the property is sold, proceeds from the sale are wired to the Qualified Intermediary for holding while a replacement property is identified and purchased.

The "1031 Exchanger" chooses a property and identifies it as their replacement property within a 45 day time frame after the close of the relinquished property, as per the IRS rules.

The "1031 Exchanger" completes the transaction within the 180-day window required bu the IRS, by closing on the Stratton Investments replacement property they have previously identified.
